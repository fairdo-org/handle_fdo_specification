PID & Resolution

[H1-1] PID System
Each Handle-based FDO has a Handle identifier as its PID, assigned through the Handle System.
[H1-2] PID Detection
According to [[RFC3650]], all Handle PIDs conform to this schema: 
<Handle Naming Authority> "/" <Handle Local Name>
The 'Handle Naming Authority' part of the Handle PID is also called a Handle prefix, is typically assigned to an organization, and MUST be unique. The 'Handle Local Name' part of the Handle PID is also called a Handle suffix and must be unique within a Handle prefix. According to [[RFC3650]], "Handles may consist of any printable characters from the Universal Character Set (UCS-2) of ISO/IEC 10646" with [[UTF-8]] encoding. In practice, the syntax of a Handle PID follows the regular expression: 
^([0-9A-Za-z])+.([0-9A-Za-z])+/([!-~])+$
[H1-3] PID Resolution
The resolution of a Handle-based FDO, which is identified by PID X, into an FDO record uses the global Handle Resolver to receive the Handle record at `https://hdl.handle.net/api/handles/[X]`. This is further specified in [[RFC3652]] and [[HandleTechnical]].
